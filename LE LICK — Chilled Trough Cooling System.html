3. Asymmetric J-Profile Design

One solid piece · Short wall on chain side · Stick passes over the top

Key insight: The chain-side wall is SHORT — just below where the stick enters the candy. The stick passes OVER the top with 3–5 mm clearance. The far wall stays TALL to fully cradle the candy. This J-shaped profile is one solid piece: no slot, no structural weakness, simple to fabricate from a single aluminum extrusion.

Fabrication is simple: This is a single piece of aluminum — either CNC-machined from a billet, or welded/brazed from flat stock. The J-profile is just a concave cradle with one wall taller than the other. Coolant channels can be drilled or brazed on. No slots, no moving parts, no structural compromises. A competent machine shop could make this in a day.

Non-Stick Surface

The inner trough surface needs to be either PTFE (Teflon) coated aluminum, polished 304/316 stainless steel, or lined with UHMW-PE. All food-grade. PTFE is best for release — even if the candy briefly contacts the surface, it won't stick.

Height Adjustment

Mounting brackets should have slotted holes for vertical adjustment. Dial in the gap between candy and trough surface — too close and you get sticking or marking, too far and you lose the contact/radiant cooling benefit.

7. Cooling Methods

Four approaches for chilling the trough, from proof-of-concept to production-grade

Recirculating Glycol Chiller RECOMMENDED

A small recirculating chiller pumps food-grade propylene glycol/water mix (35-40°F) through channels in the trough. Continuous flow means consistent temperature with a 200°F+ differential against the candy.

Cost: $800-2,500 · 1/4-1/2 HP unit · 3/8" food-grade silicone tubing · Setup: 2-4 hours

Ice Water Bath CHEAPEST TEST

Weld a sealed chamber to the trough underside and run ice water through via a submersible pump in a cooler. Perfect for same-day proof-of-concept validation before investing in a real chiller.

Cost: $50-100 · 33-45°F · Runtime: 1-2 hours per ice fill · Perfect for first trial

Thermoelectric (Peltier) Plates NO PLUMBING

Bond TEC modules directly to the aluminum trough. No water, no plumbing, no chiller. Limited cooling capacity but works well for small contact areas or as supplemental cooling.

Cost: $200-500 · ~15-40W per module · May struggle at production speed

Vortex Tube USES EXISTING AIR

If you have shop compressed air, a vortex tube splits it into cold (-20°F to +20°F) and hot streams. Pipe the cold stream into a sealed chamber under the trough. No water, no electricity.

Cost: $150-400 · 10-20 CFM at 80-100 PSI · 1,000-5,000 BTU/hr · Reliable, no moving parts
